About CouplesCash

Building stronger relationships through transparent, shared financial management.

We believe that money conversations shouldn't be awkward. CouplesCash makes joint finances simple, transparent, and stress-free for modern couples.

Our Mission

Money is the leading cause of relationship stress for couples. We're changing that by creating the first banking platform designed specifically for partners who want to share their financial lives without giving up their independence.

CouplesCash empowers couples to make financial decisions together while maintaining individual autonomy. Our platform provides the tools, insights, and transparency needed to build a stronger financial foundation for your relationship.

Our Story

CouplesCash was born from a personal frustration. Our founders, Alex and Jamie, were a couple struggling to manage their shared expenses while maintaining separate bank accounts. They tried spreadsheets, expense-splitting apps, and even a traditional joint account, but nothing felt quite right.

Traditional joint accounts felt like giving up financial independence. Separate accounts with splitting apps created awkward "who pays for what?" conversations. There had to be a better way.

After talking to hundreds of couples, they realized they weren't alone. Modern relationships needed modern banking solutions. So in 2023, they set out to build the bank account they wished they'd had.

Why Couples Need This

Traditional banking wasn't designed for modern relationships. Here's what we learned from talking to thousands of couples:

  • Financial transparency builds trust. Couples who can see each other's spending patterns have fewer money fights.
  • Shared goals motivate better habits. Saving for a vacation together is more motivating than saving alone.
  • Small purchases don't need approval. But big ones benefit from partner input.
  • Money conversations should be ongoing. Not just during monthly "budget meetings."
  • Each person needs some autonomy. Individual spending freedom within agreed boundaries.
